# Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2026-06-29

omyscan is a public alpha tool by Dr.PyLab for mapping agent-facing exposure signals on public web targets.

## What we collect

When you use the hosted demo, we may process:

- the target URL or hostname you submit for scanning;
- scan metadata such as total findings, visible findings, locked findings, categories, timestamps and request status;
- technical request data needed for rate limiting, abuse prevention and service reliability;
- your email address if you choose to request early access to the Extended Report.

We do not require an account for the public alpha.

## What we do not collect intentionally

omyscan does not ask for passwords, API keys, authentication tokens, private documents or source code.

Do not submit confidential, internal or private URLs.

## How scans work

Hosted scans are passive. omyscan uses GET/HEAD requests against a limited set of public, allowlisted paths. It does not fuzz, exploit, brute-force, bypass authentication or crawl arbitrary paths.

## How we use data

We use collected data to:

- run the requested scan;
- improve scan reliability and product quality;
- measure interest in the Extended Report;
- prevent abuse and protect the hosted service;
- contact users who requested early access.

## Early access emails

If you submit your email for early access, we may use it to contact you about omyscan, Extended Reports, product updates or related Dr.PyLab services.

You can request removal from the early access list by contacting the project maintainers.

## Logs and retention

During the alpha period, logs may be retained for debugging, abuse prevention and product analytics. We aim to avoid storing full URLs with sensitive query parameters where host-level data is sufficient.

## Third-party services

The hosted version may be deployed through infrastructure providers such as hosting platforms, DNS providers, analytics or logging services. These providers may process technical data needed to deliver the service.

## Security

If you find a security issue in omyscan, please follow the process described in SECURITY.md.

## Changes

This policy may change as omyscan evolves from public alpha to production.
